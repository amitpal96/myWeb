About Me

I am a research scholar at NISER, India. My research focuses on Neutrino physics. Neutrinos are fundamental particles that make up the universe. Every second, millions of them pass through our fingertips, yet we don't feel them. Why? Because they rarely interact with matter. In fact, a neutrino might interact with our body only once in an entire lifetime! This makes detecting them an enormous challenge.

But why do we study them? Because understanding neutrinos can unlock secrets about the origins and evolution of the universe. Despite the difficulty in detecting them, scientists have developed advanced detectors to observe these elusive particles. One such experiment is NOvA at Fermilab, USA, where I contribute to research on neutrino properties. Fermilab not only detects neutrinos but also produces them in controlled environments, helping us study their behavior in detail. My work with NOvA is part of this global effort to decode the mysteries of the universe through neutrino physics.
